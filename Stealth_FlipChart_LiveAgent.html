NODE 01FIRST CALL
START — Connection Stage
NEPQ: Connection | Voss: Barrel-Through Hook + Soft Check-In | Tone: COLLABORATIVE
Three-beat structure: (1) Identity + Hook — deliver the value proposition before asking for anything. (2) Context Frame — position as notification, not solicitation. (3) Soft Check-In — first diagnostic window for personality detection. Their response to the check-in is the first data point.
[UNDETERMINED] — Default Barrel-Through Opening
DETECTION WINDOW: Their response to the check-in is diagnostic data. Listen for pace, length, tone, and what they say first.
AGENT
Hi [CLIENT NAME], this is [AGENT NAME] with Stealth Investigations — I'm reaching out because our research team flagged a payment in your name from [REPORTING COMPANY] that never made it to you.
AGENT
I wanted to make sure you were aware of it.
AGENT
Does that ring a bell at all?
WAIT. Do not fill the silence. Their natural response reveals type:
Short, clipped, asks about money or next steps= likely DIRECTOR
Warm, animated, launches into a story= likely SOCIALIZER
Soft, uncertain, mentions family member= likely RELATER
Asks a clarifying question before answering= likely THINKER
Confident read? Select the type now. Not sure? Stay UNDETERMINED — ENGAGEMENT has a built-in diagnostic question.
[DIRECTOR] — Fast, Fact-Dense, Zero Filler
Tone: AUTHORITATIVE + SERIOUS/HONEST | Pace: Match theirs — fast. Include the dollar amount upfront. No filler.
AGENT
Hi [NAME], [AGENT] with Stealth Investigations. Our research team found a payment of [AMOUNT] in your name from [COMPANY] that never reached you. I wanted to make sure you knew about it — does that ring a bell?
One breath. Straight to the check-in. No "uh," no casualness.
[SOCIALIZER] — Warm, Energetic, Hook as Story Teaser
Tone: COLLABORATIVE + PLAYFUL | Pace: Upbeat, match their energy.
AGENT
Hey [NAME]! It's [AGENT] over at Stealth Investigations — listen, I've got something interesting for you. Our research team found a payment in your name from [COMPANY] that never made it to you. I wanted to make sure you knew about it — does that ring a bell at all?
"Something interesting for you" is a teaser that plays to their curiosity. If they go on a tangent, allow 15-20 seconds before redirecting to ENGAGEMENT.
[RELATER] — Softer Delivery, Unhurried, Reassuring Frame
Tone: COLLABORATIVE + CONCERNED | Pace: Slow, warm, gentle.
AGENT
Hi [NAME], this is [AGENT] with Stealth Investigations. I'm calling because our research team came across some information I think would be important for you to know about — it looks like there's a payment in your name from [COMPANY] that never reached you. Does that ring a bell at all?
"Important for you to know about" frames the call as looking out for them. Slower pace prevents the barrel-through from feeling aggressive.
[THINKER] — Structured, Credentialed, Specific
Tone: AUTHORITATIVE + SERIOUS/HONEST | Pace: Measured, deliberate.
AGENT
Hi [NAME], this is [AGENT] with Stealth Investigations. I'm calling because our research department identified a payment in your name — it was reported by [COMPANY] and is currently being held by the State of Texas. I wanted to make sure you were aware of it. Does that ring a bell?
"Research department identified" and "currently being held by the State of Texas" add specificity. The extra detail signals credibility before they have to ask for it.

ENGAGEMENT — Problem Awareness
NEPQ: Situation + Problem Awareness | Voss: Labeling + Accusation Audit | Tone: CONCERNED → COLLABORATIVE
Structure: Deliver the news, pause for natural reaction (Detection Moment 1), brief acknowledgment, then meta-preference question (Detection Moment 2). By the end of this node the agent should have enough data to select a personality type.

Meta-preference question: "Just so I can make sure I cover everything the right way for you — when something like this comes up, do you usually like to get all the details upfront, or do you prefer just the highlights and you'll ask if you need more?" This serves as both the personality diagnostic AND the delivery instruction for CONSEQUENCE.
[UNDETERMINED] — Pause-React-Transition + Meta-Preference
Deliver at moderate pace. Not rushed. Not slow. Watch for reactions throughout.
AGENT
We've been researching a case involving [REPORTING COMPANY], and according to the notes on our file, it looks like they tried to issue you a payment in the amount of [AMOUNT] back in [YEAR — always subtract 2 years from reporting year].
AGENT
I believe we sent you a notification about this a couple of weeks ago — it was sent to your address on [STREET NAME]...
AGENT
Were you aware that [REPORTING COMPANY] failed to deliver your payment [X] years ago?
PAUSE. Do not fill the silence. Their natural reaction is Detection Moment 1:
Brief, action-oriented ("So what do we do about it?")= DIRECTOR
Emotional, expressive ("Oh my God, are you kidding me?!")= SOCIALIZER
Worried, deferent ("Oh no... I should probably tell my husband")= RELATER
Asks follow-up questions ("What was the exact date? What type of payment?")= THINKER
AGENT
I completely understand.
OR: "I'm glad I was able to reach you about this." Match your acknowledgment to their emotional register.
AGENT
Just so I can make sure I cover everything the right way for you — when something like this comes up, do you usually like to get all the details upfront, or do you prefer just the highlights and you'll ask if you need more?
Detection Moment 2 — their answer tells you both personality AND how to deliver CONSEQUENCE:
"Highlights" / "Just the short version"= DIRECTOR — run condensed CONSEQUENCE
"Oh, tell me everything!" / enthusiastic= SOCIALIZER — run full narrative CONSEQUENCE
"Whatever you think is best" / defers to you= RELATER — run empathy-weighted CONSEQUENCE
"All the details, please" / requests specifics= THINKER — run data-rich CONSEQUENCE
[DIRECTOR] — Facts First, No Emotional Setup
Tone: CONCERNED, brisk. Deliver facts. Skip the emotional framing. Get to the awareness question fast.
AGENT
We've been researching a case involving [REPORTING COMPANY]. According to our file, they tried to issue you [AMOUNT] back in [YEAR] — it was sent to your address on [STREET NAME]. Were you aware they failed to deliver that payment?
Compress the setup into one or two sentences. The Director wants the fact, not the story.
AGENT
When something like this comes up, do you usually like to get all the details or just the highlights?
They will almost certainly say highlights. Confirm. Move to condensed CONSEQUENCE.
[SOCIALIZER] — Build Curiosity, "Wait Until You Hear This"
Tone: COLLABORATIVE + CONCERNED. Build anticipation before the reveal. Let them react and vent after.
AGENT
So I've been looking into a case involving [REPORTING COMPANY], and wait until you hear this — according to our file, it looks like they tried to issue you [AMOUNT] back in [YEAR], sent to your address on [STREET NAME].
PAUSE after the amount. Let them react. This is a natural story beat.
AGENT
Were you aware that [REPORTING COMPANY] never got that payment to you?
Let them vent if they want. Give them 15-20 seconds before the acknowledgment.
AGENT
I hear you. Listen — just so I can walk you through this in a way that's useful to you, do you usually like all the details upfront or just the highlights and you'll ask questions as we go?
[RELATER] — Gentle, Empathetic, Unhurried
Tone: CONCERNED, warm, slow. The news may upset them. Be the steady, caring voice.
AGENT
So we've been researching a case involving [REPORTING COMPANY], and I want to make sure I handle this carefully because it's a bit of an unexpected situation. According to our notes, it looks like they tried to issue you a payment of [AMOUNT] back in [YEAR] — it was sent to your address on [STREET NAME].
AGENT
Were you aware that [REPORTING COMPANY] failed to deliver that to you?
PAUSE. If they sound worried or uncertain, the acknowledgment is critical:
AGENT
I completely understand. Please don't worry — we're going to go through everything step by step.
AGENT
Just so I can make sure I cover this the right way for you — do you usually prefer to get all the details upfront, or would you rather I give you the main points and you can ask questions as we go?
[THINKER] — Structured, Specific, Sequenced
Tone: AUTHORITATIVE + CONCERNED. Provide dates, amounts, and specifics upfront. They want data, not narrative.
AGENT
I'm calling in reference to a case we've been researching involving [REPORTING COMPANY]. According to our records, they tried to issue a payment of [EXACT AMOUNT] back in approximately [YEAR], originally sent to [REPORTED ADDRESS]. It was never delivered, and the funds were subsequently turned over to the State of Texas.
AGENT
Were you aware that [REPORTING COMPANY] failed to deliver that payment?
PAUSE. They will likely ask a follow-up question before answering — that is normal. Answer it directly.
AGENT
Just so I can structure this conversation efficiently for you — do you prefer to get all the relevant details upfront, or would you like me to give you the highlights and you can drill down where you have questions?
They will almost certainly say all the details. Confirm. Move to data-rich CONSEQUENCE.

[RELATER] — Gentle, Empathetic, Steady Pace
Slow down. This prospect may be emotionally affected by the news. Your job is to be the steady, reassuring voice throughout.

AGENT
I'm really sorry to be sharing this with you. I know this kind of thing is frustrating to hear. But I'm genuinely glad that we were able to reach you so at least you know what's going on.
AGENT
So if I'm understanding this right — [COMPANY] owed you [AMOUNT], they never reached out to you, and now the State has the money. Does that sound right to you?
Soft pause after the summary. Let them confirm at their own pace.
[THINKER] — Factual, Detailed, Structured
Precision matters to this prospect. Provide dates, amounts, and entity names with specificity. No emotional amplification.
AGENT

Under Texas Property Code Chapter 74, the funds were then turned over to the State of Texas, which is the standard process after the statutory dormancy period.
AGENT
The reporting company did not make additional contact attempts before reporting it, which is where the breakdown occurred on their end.
AGENT
So to confirm my understanding — [COMPANY] issued [AMOUNT] in [YEAR], did not contact you prior to reporting it, and the funds are now held by the State. Is that accurate?
If they correct anything, note it and adjust. Thinkers respect accuracy and will respect you for welcoming their correction.

Delivery calibration comes from the meta-preference answer given in ENGAGEMENT. If you do not have a confirmed type by now, use UNDETERMINED. The sarcastic document requirements line is a diagnostic tool — how they react to it will almost always reveal their type if you still do not have a read.
[UNDETERMINED] — Medium-Length Delivery, Watch for Reactions
Deliver the core script at medium length. Not the full Socializer narrative. Not the condensed Director version. Include the sarcastic document requirements line — it draws reactions that reveal type.
AGENT
So it seems like anytime you're dealing with any governmental agency, they always make their process much more difficult than it actually should be...
AGENT
First there's the initial forms... a couple of signatures required... no big deal there...
AGENT

DETECTION WINDOW — Their reaction to the absurd requirements line:
Cuts in with "So how long does YOUR process take?"= DIRECTOR — select and condense
Laughs or reacts dramatically ("You're KIDDING!")= SOCIALIZER — lean in and continue with narrative
Sounds stressed or overwhelmed ("Oh my... that's a lot")= RELATER — soften and emphasize empathy

AGENT

AGENT

AGENT
...birth certificates, death certificates, marriage licenses, divorce decrees... and my personal favorite — proof of your address from 1984 and a copy of a bank statement from 2003. I mean...

And here's the crazy part — if you don't give them every single thing on their list, on time? Denied. Or suspended. That's it. And THAT is where most people just... give up. Which is apparently the whole point.
AGENT

AGENT
Three months of work. Just to find out you're starting over. Most people are gone by that point.

Emphasize the personal burden. Imagine going through this alone, taking time from family, dealing with bureaucracy that does not care about you.
AGENT
So let me walk you through what the State requires, because I want you to understand what you're dealing with before you decide anything...

AGENT

AGENT

PAUSE. Gentle pause — not dramatic. Just let the reality land before moving to SOLUTION.

AGENT

AGENT

AGENT
Total timeline for the average DIY claimant: six to nine months, assuming no denials or suspension. Most people abandon the process after the first document request. The State pays out less than three percent of held funds annually.
PAUSE. Let the data register. Move to SOLUTION.

NODE 15OBJECTIONS
INTERESTED_NOT_SURE — Dig for Real Objection
Voss: Label + Tactical Silence | Tone: COLLABORATIVE
After the binary choice, this is the silence node. The label ("It SEEMS like you have a really good reason for saying that") is a question disguised as a statement. Upward inflection at the end. Then absolute silence. The prospect will fill the silence with their real objection.
[UNDETERMINED] — Core Label + Silence Protocol
AGENT
Okay, Interested But Not Sure... It SEEMS like you have a really good reason for saying that?
Upward inflection. Then TOTAL SILENCE. Count to 10 in your head. Do not speak first. The prospect will fill the void.
DETECTION — how they handle the silence:
Gives you a direct answer immediately= DIRECTOR
Starts talking and tells you a story about their hesitation= SOCIALIZER
Hesitates, sounds uncomfortable, eventually gives a vague answer= RELATER
Asks a clarifying question before answering= THINKER
If they give a real objection: "Oh, okay... I think [REPEAT THEIR EXACT OBJECTION] is a legitimate reason/concern." Then route. If they do not give a real objection, proceed to systematic probing: Probe 1 (Credibility), Probe 2 (Fee), Probe 3 (DIY).
[DIRECTOR] — Shorter Silence Tolerance, Move Fast if Silence Extends
AGENT
Interested But Not Sure... It seems like you have a good reason for that?
If silence extends past 5 seconds: "What's the holdup?" — direct, not harsh. They will respect the directness.
[SOCIALIZER] — They Will Fill Silence Quickly, Let the Story Come Out
AGENT
Okay, Interested But Not Sure... It seems like something's on your mind?
They will fill the silence almost immediately with a story, an explanation, or a concern framed as a narrative. Let them finish completely before responding. Do not interrupt. The more they talk, the more you learn.
[RELATER] — They May Need Gentle Encouragement to Share
AGENT
Okay, Interested But Not Sure... It seems like there might be something you're not quite sure about?
Hold the silence for 8 to 10 seconds. If they still have not answered, a gentle prompt is acceptable:
AGENT
It's okay to share — I just want to make sure I'm being helpful.
[THINKER] — Silence Is Processing, Not Discomfort — Hold Longer
AGENT
Interested But Not Sure... It seems like you have a specific reason for that?
Hold silence for a full 12 to 15 seconds if needed. A Thinker's silence is analytical — they are organizing their thoughts. Breaking it early signals impatience and will cost you. When they do speak, their objection will be specific and will likely require a specific response. Be ready.

IF they give a real objection → route to CREDIBILITY_OBJECTION, FEE_OBJECTION, or DIY_OBJECTION based on content
IF no real objection surfaced → Probe 1: CREDIBILITY_OBJECTION
IF Probe 1 is not the issue → Probe 2: FEE_OBJECTION
IF Probe 2 is not the issue → Probe 3: DIY_OBJECTION
IF none of the above → UNKNOWN_OBJECTION
